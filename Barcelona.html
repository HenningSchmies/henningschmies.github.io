As our last station, we wanted to pass some days in Barcelona, so we booked a nice looking AirBnb and organised everything else. We flew in from Porto, and originally, the plan was that Isabel would return to Madrid and I would be flying out from Girona back to Germany.

The Highlight

The best moment of out journey in Barcelona, for me, was out visit to Parque Güell. To make this visit one has to order tickets online in advance for a certain entry hour, and they are relatively strict about that. I guess the unlying logic is to avoid Parque Güell being to crowded. Apparently, they are also rather strict about eating in the parque.

First of all, to get an impression of what's there to see in Barcelona take a look at this quite boring yet nice to look at video of Parque Güell.

Accommodation

Auf Airbnb ansehenDouble room in 90m2 flat

So, not let's take a look at our AirBnb: The AirBnb was essentially the first one we booked without any real previous experience. In hindsight the choice could be considered a mistake, but it wasn't so horrible after all (if you asked me, at least :) ) The one difference was that we chose to book a shared apartment, so in essence we only had one room to ourselves, where during the previous stays we always had an entire, seperated apartment to us. The point really was that the circumstances weren't appeasing due to a lack of ...

We managed to stay there and weren't that much at home anyways so in the end it did not affect us that much. It has to been said though that the place was really nicely integrated into the metro system and in walking distance to La Sagrada Familia, at least if you like walking.
